Who Doesn't Love a Good Wine?¶

There are varying levels of knowledge and appreciation when it comes to drinking wine¶

Several factors come into play when people are selecting a bottle of wine -- location, year bottled, type of grape, price¶

Do these factors actually influence the popularity of various wine brands?¶

Our Group Questions¶

1. Year Bottled vs. Rating -- What effect does the year the wine was bottled have on it's popularity rating?¶

2. Wine Price vs. Rating -- What effect does the price of a bottle of wine have on it's popularity rating?¶

3. Year Bottled vs. Wine Price -- What effect does the year the wine was bottled have on it's price?¶

4. What countries produce the most expensive wine?¶

5. What countries produce the most highly rated wine?¶

# Call the Google Geocode API to retrieve lat and lng for the countries in the DataFrame
wine_country["lat"] = ""
wine_country["lng"] = ""

params = {"key": g_key}


for index, row in wine_country.iterrows():   
    
    base_url = "https://maps.googleapis.com/maps/api/geocode/json"
    
    country = index
    
    params["address"] = f"{country}"
    
    country_lat_lng = requests.get(base_url, params=params)

    # Convert to JSON
    country_geo = country_lat_lng.json()

    # Extract lat/lng
    wine_country.loc[index, "lat"] = country_geo["results"][0]["geometry"]["location"]["lat"]
    wine_country.loc[index, "lng"] = country_geo["results"][0]["geometry"]["location"]["lng"]

Conclusions we found through our analysis!¶

The country with the highest average price of wine is Switzerland with an average bottle of wine priced at 85.25 dollars.¶



The next country with the highest average price of wine is 32.85 dollars below (England), followed by Germany for price.¶




The average price of wine for all countries analyzed is 26.64 dollars.¶

Average wine rating for all countries analyzed is 87.735 with a range of 7.9.¶

Ukraine recorded the lowest rated wine and also the cheapest wine.¶

The country with the highest rated wine does not have the highest priced wine.¶

India represented a potential outlier in our data, with a 90.2 average wine rating and an average price of 13.3 dollars.¶

According to the linear regression equation, the average cost of a bottle of wine in India should have been around 48 dollars.¶

Post Mortem¶

We were limited in the fact that our dataset only showed Provinces. Visually, a gmap showing the cities where the best rated and priced wines are would have been nice to show.¶

Our correlations were not very strong. Running an ANOVA test would have allowed us to view whether or not there was any statistical significance in the relationship of Wine and Rating.¶
